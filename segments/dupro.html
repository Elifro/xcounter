Reliable, Repeatable Imaging for Production Line Inspection

Whilst the range of applications for using X-ray imaging in Production quality control is very large, at the heart of all these process investigations is the need for repeatable and reliable results with the minimum of throughput intervention. XCounter photon counting detectors deliver market leading detector performance without suffering from the ghosting or lag issues of many TFT flat panel detectors. This means that with our detectors you are able to establish an X-ray imaging system into your quality examination without compromising on your production speeds. And the ground breaking fast rate XCounter detectors running at up to 20,000fps which is equivalent to imaging at 2m/s, brings you the opportunity to retrofit an XCounter detector into an existing X-ray imaging system and potentially increase your process speed.

The environmental resilience of our detectors make them non-sensitive to extreme changes in temperature and humidity whilst dormant. And their resilience whilst imaging is supported by proprietary software solutions such as Real-time Temperature Correction (RTC). This robustness makes our detectors ideal for the industrial environment.

Superior Performance Integrated into your Process

With the broad range of requirements of production inspection, the depth of capability of our detectors make them suitable for most quality control applications. With analysis using high energy X-rays, the stopping power of CdTe brings detector longevity to your testing system; some XCounter group detectors have been in operation for ten years. With low energy imaging applications, Detector Quantum Efficiency (DQE) of 85% @ RQA5 spectra and a Modulation Transfer Function (MTF) of >80% @ 2lp/mm provide images of the highest spatial resolution for the lowest dose exposure. For requirements needing material differentiation, the powerful dual-energy abilities of all of our detectors, give superior performance.

Geometric configurability of our three side buttable detectors, bring a range of diverse examination windows: from long length line-scan to compact block imaging. Whether the application is tiny weld examination on nuclear fuel rods, a Li-ion battery QC or an Integrated circuit inspection at 20micron voxels a geometric detector arrangement can be found to suit your configuration.

Software and hardware triggers, and adaptable in-house software design provide our development team with the tools to support you in detector integration; both facilitating smooth retro-fit solutions and de-risking new system innovation. The XCounter group detectors have, over many years, worked with production Quality Control applications around the world, from mining to raw material processing; paper mills to X-ray tube producers. Whatever you produce, if you need to X-ray image it, our detectors will deliver unparalleled performance.

Technology

XCounter has a solid foundation in both integrating and photon counting state of the art x-ray detector technology.

The basic component in our detectors are a conversion material, which converts the x-rays into electrical signals, and a CMOS (ASIC) which transforms the electric signals into a datastream of either the integrated electric charge or the number of photons which has entered each pixel. This data is then used to create a digital image which is either two- or threedimensional, depending on the scan geometry.

Our solid state detector uses CdTe which is a direct conversion material. When an x-ray photon enters the material it is converted directly to an electric signal which is captured, measured and finally processed into an image. This provides significantly better MTF (Modulation Transfer Function) and DQE (Detection Quantum Efficiency) compared to scintillation detectors due to the high sensitivity and very good stopping power for high energy photons. By combining the CdTe conversion layer with our custom ASICs (CMOS) we can supply detectors that support integrating as well as photon counting modes, fast readout and low power consumption.
